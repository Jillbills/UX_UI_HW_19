Edúcate Ya Case Study

Responsive Website Redesign

What is Edúcate Ya?

Edúcate Ya is a non-profit organization that helps educate Latinos to achieve better jobs and access to healthcare. We redesigned the non-profit organization’s website to be responsive and visually engaging to improve usability, navigation, and user engagement.



Project Overview

The Problem

Edúcate Ya hasn’t been reaching it’s targeted audience. Their homepage averaged an 85% bounce rate in Oct to Nov 2021, which is nearly twice as high as a typical website bounce rate.

The Solution

By redesigning Edúcate Ya’s website to be more visually engaging, we can help improve usability, navigation, and user engagement.

Research Plan

Planned to interview potential users of the website and stakeholders to determine what they would like to see on the website. Use insights to create the best website possible for the organization.

Our UX Research team conducted 3 stakeholder interviews and 4 interviews with potential users. It was semi-structured group and 1:1 interviews conducted by phone, over social media, and over Zoom.

Potential User Interviews + Stakeholder Interviews
= Key User Insights


  • Until April 2022, Edúcate Ya is offering free English classes to Latinos. This should be more prominent on the website - NONE of the potential users we interviewed noticed it.

  • Edúcate Ya relies on donor funding. The website has a modal that shows up initially, but the timeing is not ideal. The user should not need to look for the Call to Action (CTA) button.

  • Provide users control to select a preferred language that switches the entire copy to that one language consistently throughout the website.

  • Users liked how easy it was to find information on the website. Build upon this by improving the visual design & strategically highlighting the organization's goals.

Definition

Problem Statement

By redesigning Edúcate Ya’s website to be more visually engaging, we can help increase engagement with and participation in the organization’s classes and events.

We also believe that a website that is easier to maintain for the organization and more delightful for people outside of the organization to use will result in an increase in donations to Edúcate Ya.


UX Hypothesis

The primary user of Edúcate Ya’s website is not typically the direct user of their education and leadership programs.

Instead, the primary user might be someone who has better Internet access and proficiency navigating online resources that tells the direct user by word of mouth or refers them to the website.

This hypothesis later
proved to be true!

Conclusion

Reflections

  • Learning more about the actual users (18 to 24 year old demographic) from the Google Analytics report helped us redesign the style of the Edúcate Ya website to appeal to the appropriate targeted audience.

  • It was very rewarding to see that the stakeholders began implementing some of our redesign recommendations soon after our team presented it to them!

Future Developments

  • Increase copy font size for mobile to at least 16px for better readability

  • Implementing a “Back to Top” button, which is currently designed but not active yet
